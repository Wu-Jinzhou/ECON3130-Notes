margin of sampling error is ¬±3 percentage points.


    Interpretation:
        ‚Ä¢ Suppose WP/GMU conducts 100 polls of 1107 adults
        ‚Ä¢ And suppose, in each case, we constructed our sample average
          and SE in this fashion.
        ‚Ä¢ Then, in about 95 cases out of 100, the true (unknown) percentage
          of the population supporting Youngkin would be within the (2 SE)
          margin of error ‚Äì which is about 3 percentage points.
        ‚Ä¢ Or, in only about 5 out of the 100 cases, would the true (unknown)
