Two Sample Test (small samples)
When the sample sizes are small, the Central Limit Theorem doesn’t apply,
  so our test statistic might not have a normal distribution under the null
  hypothesis.
BUT IF our data are approximately normal AND the observations are
  independent AND the variance is the same both populations, then our test
  statistic has a t distribution with n1+n2-2 degrees of freedom.

Paired vs. independent samples
Paired samples:
  • Pairs of comparable individuals are selected where one is the
   “treatment” and the other is the “control”
    • Matches can be selected based on criteria like age and sex
  • In a longitudinal study, the same group of people is followed over time
    • The early (pre-treatment) measure is considered the control and the later
      (post-treatment) measure is the treatment
    • Test of whether difference is zero is really a one sample test.
  • Advantages:
    • Generally lower standard errors for estimate of the difference between the
      groups relative to independent samples of the same size
Independent samples:
  • More common with observational data
  • Standard errors tend to be higher than with paired samples because
   there is more variation not due to the treatment between the two
   samples

Up until now, our one sample tests have looked like this:

          H0:   µ = µ0
          H1:   µ ≠ µ0

We call these two sided tests because under the alternative hypothesis, µ may
be less than or greater than some number.

Sometimes we may be willing to assume µ can never be positive (or negative).
This is a one-sided test.

          H1:   D >0

Sometimes we may be willing to assume mu can never be positive (or negative). This is a

e.g., Suppose we think it is impossible that Ritalin could decrease VISP latency. D is the

            H0 :   D =0

What does change is our conception of an extreme value.

Now, our p-value is Pr(T>t) instead of Pr(T>|t| or T<-|t|).

In general, p-values for a one-sided test are half the size of those for the corresponding
two-sided test. It is easier to reject H0 with a one-sided test.

The conservative approach is to always use a two-sided test.
